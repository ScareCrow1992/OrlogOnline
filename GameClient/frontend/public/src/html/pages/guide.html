Orlog

Orlog is a dice game popular among the Norse and Anglo-Saxons in which two players duel by rolling dice and invoking Godfavors. The first player to reduce the Health of their opponent to 0 wins.

1. Game Play

A game of orlog consists of a number of rounds between two players. Before the game begins, players flip a coin to determine who moves first. Each round has three phases: Roll, God Favor, and Resolution.

1-1. Roll Phase

In the Roll phase, the two players take turns rolling their individual sets of dice. After each roll, players chooses which dice to keep, if any, and which to reroll. A player may reroll some or all of the dice up to two times on this phase.

Before the end of the each turn, players can offer additional silver coin to the opponent through their own doubling cube.

1-2. Godfavor Phase

In the God Favor phase, each player can choose to cast a blessing from a Godfavor in Norse mythology, or may skip it entirely. Players can pick blessings from up to a total of three figures, and can increase the variety of blessings available to them by successfully beating select challengers across Norway and England.

The blessing costs vary in their amount of favor, which is represented by the symbol ⌘ and is accumulated across multiple rounds. The blessings take affect during the Resolution phase, with some blessings having priority in the sequence over others.

At the end of Godfavor Phase, the silver coin declared in Roll Phase is reflected in the current game.

1-3. Resolution Phase

In the Resolution phase, the previously chosen powers of God are invoked, and the battle of dice begins. each players' set of dice faces are compared with damage blocked or dealt, and favor gained or stolen. The game is over when a player's health reaches zero.

4. Game Modes

There are three types of game modes : Constant, Liberty, Draft

The rules for each game mode are mostly the same. The only difference is how you select the card to use during the game.

4-1. Constant

Both players use the following God Favors: Odin, Thor, Idun.

4-2. Liberty

Each player picks 3 God Favor cards from the 20 available.

4-3. Draft

  1. Every turns, Two players take choosing cards one for each to ban and pick.
  2. Start with the first player, but can't pick a card on the first turn. (Ban only)
  3. Repeat the ban-pick until 3 cards are in each player's hand.

5. Scoring

Points are a measure of your gaming skill and can be found by the figures on the right side of the Start Game button.

The winner basically gets 5 point. and add extra points. And earns extra points as much as HP remains. The loser loses the points the winner gets.

For example, if the game ends with 8 HP left, the result will be settled with 13 points, which is the sum of 5 (default points) and 8 (HP points).

In case of surrender, the HP remaining for the winner will be settled at that moment.

If the result after settlement is less than 100, the point will not go down even if you lose the game.
